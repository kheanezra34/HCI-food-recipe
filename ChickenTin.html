Chicken Tinola


Chicken Tinola, is a classic Filipino comfort soup, perfect for a chilly day or when you're feeling under the weather. Its a popular Filipino soup, known for its comforting, flavorful broth made with chicken, ginger, garlic, and fish sauce, often including vegetables like papaya and malunggay leaves.



Ingredients:

  • 3 lbs bone-in chicken, cut into serving pieces (Note: Boneless chicken can be substituted, but bone-in is traditional for richer flavor. You can remove the skin for a lower-fat option.)

  • 1 large onion, chopped

  • 3 thumbs ginger, julienned (about 3 inches)

  • 2 green papayas, wedged (or 2 chayotes, cut into wedges – chayote is a good substitute if papaya is unavailable)
  • 1 ½ cups malunggay leaves (Moringa leaves – substitute with spinach or other leafy greens if unavailable)

  • 1 cup hot pepper leaves (Siling labuyo leaves – optional, adds a mild heat)

  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce (Patis – adjust to taste)

  • 2 Maggi Magic Chicken Cubes (optional, for extra chicken flavor)

  • 1 quart rice wash (water used to rinse uncooked rice)

  • 1 quart water (or more, as needed to adjust consistency)

  • Salt and pepper to taste

  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil

Instructions:

  1. Prepare Ingredients:Wash and prepare all ingredients as listed above.
  2. Sauté Aromatics: Heat cooking oil in a pot over medium heat. Sauté garlic, onion, and ginger until softened and fragrant.


  3. Brown Chicken: Add chicken pieces to the pot and cook for 2-3 minutes per side, until lightly browned.


  4. Add Seasoning and Rice Wash: Pour in fish sauce and stir well. Add rice wash and bring to a boil.


  5. Simmer: Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 30-45 minutes, or until chicken is tender. Add water as needed to maintain desired consistency.


  6. Add Vegetables: Add papaya (or chayote) wedges and continue to simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until tender.


  7. Add Greens & Season:Stir in Maggi cubes (if using). Add hot pepper leaves and malunggay leaves. Cover and let sit for 2 minutes to wilt the greens. Season with salt and pepper to taste.


  8. Serve:Ladle into bowls and serve hot with steamed rice. A small bowl of fish sauce and chili peppers on the side is a traditional accompaniment.
