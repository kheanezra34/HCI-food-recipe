Mango Float


Mango float, is a beloved no-bake Filipino dessert, a delightful layered treat of creamy sweetness and juicy mango. Its simple preparation belies its deliciousness, making it perfect for any occasion.



Ingredients:

  • 24 graham crackers (or ladyfingers)

  • 2 (14-ounce) cans sweetened condensed milk (chilled)

  • 4 cups heavy cream (or all-purpose cream, chilled)

  • 2-3 ripe mangoes, peeled, pitted, and sliced (Ataulfo mangoes are ideal for their
    sweetness and texture)

Instructions:

  1. Chill Ingredients: Chill the sweetened condensed milk and heavy cream overnight or for at least 2 hours. This is crucial for achieving a firm, stable whipped cream. Chilling your mixing bowl and beaters also helps.


  2. Whip the Cream: In a chilled bowl, beat the chilled heavy cream with an electric mixer until stiff peaks form. Gradually add the chilled sweetened condensed milk, continuing to beat until well combined and fluffy.


  3. Assemble the Layers: Choose a rectangular or square glass dish (approximately 8x8 inches or 9x13 inches). Line the bottom with a layer of graham crackers. Spread about 1/3 of the whipped cream mixture evenly over the crackers. Arrange a layer of mango slices on top.


  4. Repeat Layers: Repeat the layering process (crackers, cream, mangoes) two more times, ending with a layer of
    whipped cream and mango slices on top. You can arrange the top layer of mangoes attractively


  5. Chill and Serve: Cover the dish with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4-6 hours, or preferably overnight, to allow the graham crackers to soften and the flavors to meld. The longer it chills, the better the texture and flavor will be.


  6. Optional Garnish: Before serving, you can sprinkle crushed graham crackers or a dusting of powdered sugar over the top for extra texture and visual appeal


  7. Serve: Cut the Mango Float into squares and serve chilled.
