Sisig


Sisig, a popular Filipino dish,is a sizzling plate of chopped pork, typically including pig's face (maskara), ears, and sometimes cheeks, along with onions, chili peppers, and calamansi juice. Many variations exist, using pork belly as a substitute for the more traditional pig's head parts, or even substituting chicken or seafood. This recipe uses pork belly for accessibility.



Ingredients:

  • 1 lb pork belly, cut into 1-inch cubes

  • 1/2 lb chicken liver, sliced

  • large onion, finely chopped

  • 4-6 chili peppers (silang labuyo or similar), finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 cup calamansi juice (or lime/lemon juice)

  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce

  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil

  • 1 teaspoon black pepper

  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise (optional, for creamier texture)

  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Boil the Pork: Place the pork belly cubes in a pot, cover with water, and bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 45-60 minutes, or until the pork is very tender. Drain well and allow to cool slightly. This step tenderizes the meat, making it easier to chop.
  2. Grill (Optional): For a smokier flavor, grill the boiled pork cubes for a few minutes per side until lightly charred. This step is optional but adds depth of flavor.


  3. Chop the Pork: Once cool enough to handle, finely chop the pork belly. You can use a cleaver or a sharp knife.


  4. Sauté Aromatics: Heat cooking oil in a pan or wok over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until softened, about 3-5 minutes.


  5. Cook the Liver: Add the sliced chicken liver to the pan and cook until browned and cooked through, about 5-7 minutes. You can mash the liver slightly while cooking for a smoother texture.


  6. Combine Ingredients: Add the chopped pork to the pan along with the chili peppers, calamansi juice, soy sauce, and black pepper. Stir well to combine.


  7. Simmer: Reduce heat to low and simmer for 5-10 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld. Stir occasionally.


  8. Add Mayonnaise (Optional): Ladle into bowls and serve hot with steamed rice. A small bowl of fish sauce and chili peppers on the side is a traditional accompaniment.


  9. Season and Serve:Taste and adjust seasoning with salt as needed. Serve immediately on a hot plate or sizzling platter for the best presentation. Garnish with additional chili peppers and calamansi wedges.
