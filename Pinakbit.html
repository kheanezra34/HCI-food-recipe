Pinakbet


Pinakbet, is a vibrant and flavorful Filipino vegetable stew, known for its diverse mix of vegetables and savory shrimp paste-based broth.



Ingredients:

  • 1 lb pork belly, cut into 1-inch cubes

  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil

  • 1 large onion, chopped

  • 4 cloves garlic, minced

  • 2 ripe tomatoes, chopped

  • 1 small butternut squash, peeled, seeded, and cut into 1-inch cubes

  • 1 cup string beans, trimmed and cut into 1-inch pieces

  • 1 bunch okra, trimmed and halved

  • 1 medium eggplant, cut into 1-inch cubes

  • 1 bitter melon (ampalaya), seeded and cut into 1/2-inch thick slices

  • 1 ½ cups water or pork broth

  • Salt and pepper to taste

  • Optional: 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Sear the Pork: Heat oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the pork belly and cook until browned on all sides, about 5-7 minutes. Remove the pork from the pot and set aside.


  2. Sauté Aromatics: Add the chopped onions and garlic to the pot and cook until softened and fragrant, about 3-5 minutes.


  3. Add Tomatoes: Add the chopped tomatoes to the pot and cook until softened and slightly caramelized, about 5-7 minutes.


  4. Add Shrimp Paste: Stir in the shrimp paste and cook for 1 minute, allowing it to release its aroma.


  5. Add Vegetables: Add the butternut squash, string beans, okra, eggplant, and bitter melon to the pot.
    Stir well to combine.


  6. Add Liquid: Pour in the water or pork broth, ensuring all the vegetables are submerged.
    Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until
    the vegetables are tender but still retain some firmness.


  7. Add Pork: Return the seared pork belly to the pot. Stir gently to combine.


  8. Season and Serve: Season with salt and pepper to taste. Garnish with fresh cilantro (if using) and serve hot with steamed rice.
