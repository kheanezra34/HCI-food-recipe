Siomai


Siomai meaning are steamed Chinese dumplings, typically filled with ground pork, shrimp, and/or vegetables, and are a popular street food and snack in the Philippines.



Ingredients:

For the Siomai Filling:

  • 1 lb ground pork

  • 1 tablespoon whole peppercorn

  • 5 dried bay leaves

  • 2 ½ tablespoons sea salt
  • 4 cups canola oil

  • 6 cups water

      • For the Chili Garlic Oil:

    • ¼ cup vegetable oil
    • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1-2 red chilies, finely minced (adjust to your spice preference)

        • For Serving:

      • Siomai wrappers (round or square)
      • Steamed rice
      • Soy sauce (for dipping)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Chili Garlic Oil: Heat the vegetable oil in a small saucepan over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and chilies. Cook until fragrant and the garlic is lightly golden, about 1-2 minutes. Remove from heat and let cool completely.


  2. Prepare the Siomai Filling:In a large bowl, combine the ground pork, minced shrimp, onion, garlic, soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil, white pepper, and salt. Mix thoroughly until all ingredients are well combined. Stuff the chilies with the pork filling, using a spoon or piping bag if you have one.


  3. Assemble the Siomai: Place a siomai wrapper in your hand. Add about 1 tablespoon of the filling to the center. Gently gather the edges of the wrapper and pleat them to create a dumpling shape. You can find many online tutorials showing different siomai shaping techniques.


  4. Steam the Siomai: Place the assembled siomai in a steamer basket lined with parchment paper. Steam over boiling water for 12-15 minutes, or until the siomai are cooked through and the wrappers are translucent.


  5. Serve: Garnish the steamed siomai with finely chopped green onions. Drizzle with the chili garlic oil. Serve hot with steamed rice and soy sauce for dipping.
