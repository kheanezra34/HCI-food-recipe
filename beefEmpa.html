Beef Empanada


Beef Empanadas are savory pastries filled with a flavorful mixture of
ground beef, vegetables, and spices.



Ingredients:

For the Dough:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour

  • 1 teaspoon salt

  • 1 cup shortening or lard (for a richer flavor)

  • ½ cup ice water

    For the Filling:

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 medium potato, peeled and diced
  • 1 medium carrot, diced
  • 1/2 cup raisins (optional)
  • 1/4 cup green peas (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. Make the Dough: In a large bowl, combine the flour and salt. Cut in the shortening or
    lard using a pastry blender or your fingers until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
    Gradually add the ice water, mixing until the dough comes together. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes.

  2. Prepare the Filling: Heat the cooking oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the
    onion and garlic and sauté until softened. Add the ground beef and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon. Drain off any excess fat. Add the potatoes, carrots, raisins (if using), and peas (if using). Cook until the vegetables are tender. Season with salt, pepper, and soy sauce. Remove from heat and let cool completely.

  3. Assemble the Empanadas: On a lightly floured surface, roll out the dough to about ⅛
    inch thickness. Use a 4-inch round cookie cutter or a bowl to cut out circles. Place a
    spoonful of the beef filling in the center of each circle. Fold the dough over to form a
    half-moon shape, crimping the edges to seal.

    4. Bake or Fry:

    • Baking: Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Brush the empanadas with the beaten egg. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown.

    • Frying: Heat about 1 inch of oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Fry the empanadas in batches until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per side. Remove and drain on paper towels.

  4. Serve: Serve the Beef Empanadas warm. They are delicious on their own or with a
    dipping sauce of your choice (vinegar, soy sauce, or a spicy sauce).
